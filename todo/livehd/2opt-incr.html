2opt-incr Incremental synthesis — fast better/worse answers

A re-implementation of LiveSynth (DAC'17, Possignolo & Renau; UCSC thesis ch.4) on today's kernel, sharing the 2f-lec cone/digest machinery instead of re-deriving it. The purpose is evaluation speed, not tape-out: a designer (or the 2opt-freq agent loop) makes a small RTL change and needs to know is this better or worse? in seconds. Fabrication runs a normal full synthesis; the incremental engine only has to make the iteration decisions correct.

The original work's two headline costs are exactly what the landed lec.cones engine already kills: its setup was per-net SAT (3.8–15.6× the cost of a full synthesis), and its diff — a hand-rolled recursive structural walk — was 83% (fpu) to 94.8% (mips) of the live-phase runtime. A canonical structural digest turns that diff into an O(1) map lookup, and the ABC cone prover replaces the SAT setup. Both already exist and are test-proven.

Contract rulings (settled 2026-07-15)

  • This is a decision oracle, not a fabrication path. The incremental result never has to be the shipped netlist — it has to be right about the direction. A wrong "faster" wastes an iteration; a wrong "slower" prunes a good idea. Final fabrication does a full synthesis.
  • Two speeds. (1) FIB probe — ephemeral, timing-only, answers "is X faster?" in seconds, never stitched back. Its job is pruning. (2) Partition resynthesis — the durable path, run when a change is kept and always before P&R. Because the probe never stitches and never reaches P&R, FIB's area redundancy is structurally unable to leak downstream.
  • The reuse grain is a DISJOINT partition, not an overlapping cone. The 2017 area problem was inherent to the grain: single-output invariant cones overlap, so resynthesizing one cone re-emits logic shared with its neighbours (model.tex: "This procedure is sub-optimal for area, since it may result in redundancy… may accumulate over the course of multiple changes"). ICCAD reviewer #3 reached the same conclusion at the time: "I also think that treating regions as multi-output avoids duplication of gates during incremental updates." Disjoint partitions remove the refcount machinery and the accumulating drift outright.
  • Partitions are discovered iteratively and cached. Best-guess colors → parallel synthesis → check → re-partition as needed; the partition decisions are remembered so later runs do not re-iterate. Partitions pay for themselves twice: they are also the unit of parallel synthesis on the original (non-incremental) run.
  • pass.color flat remains literal. It requests one whole-design ABC region and is the full-synthesis/QoR baseline; neither pass.color nor pass.abc silently subdivides it. Instead, ABC performs memory admission before committing to the region and refuses an unsafe run with a clean diagnostic, a smaller-coloring recommendation, and an explicit pass.abc.allow_oversize=true escape hatch.
  • Timing-driven partition growth. A partition is a region timing does not likely propagate across. Synthesize it, and if the critical path runs input→output, grow the partition to absorb the neighbour and re-synthesize. (The paper's own mitigation, model.tex §Dealing with delay degradation, was the same idea applied to cones.)
  • The cache holds SIGNATURES per partition, not copies of the netlists — light, and enough to traverse and re-stitch as needed. Spec0 is never stored: a per-partition digest replaces the paper's backward traversal entirely, which is where 83–95% of its live runtime went.
  • v1 metrics: area + timing. Power deferred. Both already exist per-region today (ABC's qor.json reports area/delay; pass.opentimer does STA). Power needs switching activity from a VCD (pass.opentimer.power + vcd_power.cpp accumulate energy-per-transition), i.e. a simulation run per edit — deferred until the area/timing loop is proven.

What the lineage actually established (and did not)

  • Speed: ~10× median synthesis speedup (≈40s → ≈4s); LLIR reached only ≈20s, Quartus-II ≈120s → ≈45s. Stitch itself is milliseconds.
  • Setup cost (once per design): fpu 375s vs 37s synthesis, mips 1403s vs 90s, or1200 84s vs 22s — i.e. finding FIBs cost more than a full synthesis, in Ruby, per-net, via SAT.
  • QoR: max ≈4.5% delay degradation, none at all on ~79% of changes. Partition-size sweet spot 1k–5k gates (synthesis time is super-linear; under ~1k gates tool overhead dominates).
  • NOT established — area and power were never measured. The only QoR metric in the paper or the thesis is post-route Fmax. plots/area.gp is orphaned (it reads a ../data/area that does not exist) and area-dc.pdf is never included; the paper's own TODO asks for "Reporting Area/delay variations due to RTL changes" — never done. Area is simultaneously the metric the flow is known to bleed (stitch redundancy) and the one never checked. Any area claim here is new work, not a reproduction.
  • The old numbers are not a trustworthy target. The thesis ch.4 QoR data (chapter4/data/qor_lsynth, 84 rows) is byte-identical to the DAC17 qor_fpu+qor_mips+qor_or1200 series — i.e. it is the DAC17 data, not the Anubis data its own setup section claims, and it includes mips, which ch.3 says was excluded from Anubis.
  • Prior port: the thesis already reimplemented LiveSynth in C++14 on LGraph (LiveHD's direct ancestor) and added SMatch, a structural LUT-matching step extending reuse into P&R. This is a re-port onto the modern kernel, not a from-scratch design.

What is reused from the landed cone engine (the point of doing this now)

  • cone_digest replaces diff. The paper's Algorithm 1 is a recursive op+fanin comparison keyed on hierarchical net names — an ad-hoc, non-memoized structural hash. A 128-bit canonical DAG digest is the same question answered in O(1). This attacks the single dominant cost.
  • Canonicalization is a strict upgrade, not just a speedup. Algorithm 1 compares fanin positionally (diff(fanin(old,idx), fanin(new,idx))), so a commutative operand swap reports "changed" and needlessly enlarges the resynthesis region (SMatch has the identical weakness). A digest that commutative-normalizes shrinks regions ⇒ faster and better QoR. pass/semdiff's fold_operands already does exactly this normalization within port classes.
  • The ABC cone prover replaces the SAT setup. Proving a net's function survived synthesis is what abc_prove_unsat does; dino discharges 26 cones in 3.5s against a tech-mapped sky130 netlist.
  • The verdict cache is the signature store. formal_cache.json is already salt-gated, atomic, and holds a digest set ("cones"); partition signatures are a sibling record kind.
  • LEC is now cheap enough to gate every iteration. dino proves flat-vs-netlist in ~12s. The 2017 flow had no way to check its own output.

Landed (2026-07-16) — subtasks 0, A, C (+ B's aggregation half)

pass.abc keeps a persistent region cache under --workdir (<workdir>/abc_cache/), ON by default (--set abc.cache=false disables; no user --workdir = no cache = the lec.cache convention; --set abc.cache_dir=DIR re-points it, e.g. one cache shared across flows). The cache is content-addressed by a canonical 128-bit region digest (pass/abc/abc_incr.{hpp,cpp}). The directory holds abc_cache.json (salt-gated metadata + per-region QoR) and an lg library of previously mapped region bodies (r_<digest>). At map_region, a hit clones the cached netlist into the fresh region module — ABC never starts; a miss maps normally and snapshots. The clone is wired driver-major so a warm run's Verilog is byte-identical to the cold mapping's.

  • The digest folds: every node (op, driver widths/signs, port-grouped fanin commutative-normalized within a port class — the paper's positional-diff weakness fixed), constant values, Sub target/instance names, state names (read-back rebuilds registers by name; anonymous state REFUSES to cache — the semdiff rule), the boundary (inputs as (bits,sign) refined by fanout structure over Weisfeiler-Lehman rounds; primary inputs name-anchored; outputs as (driver sig, port, bits, sign)), and the RESOLVED per-region ABC recipe (post-region_opts flow/{D}/{L}/adder/multiplier). Liberty CONTENT, register/memory mode, dff_cell and assume flags salt the whole cache file. Ports are stitched cached→fresh through canonical boundary RANKS (sorted boundary tokens) — names and port order are nid-derived and shift under an edit, the ranks do not. Any ambiguity (two boundary pins the refinement cannot separate, duplicate output cones, comb cycle inside a region) refuses rather than guesses: a wrong reuse is a miscompile, a refusal is one ABC run. Uncacheable regions are reported with the reason per run.
  • Partition identity (A) resolved content-addressed: no name-set tracking, no re-assignment rule, no re-partition trigger — the digest IS the identity. Color renumbering, nid shifts and port renames between runs are invisible; a region "survives an edit" exactly when its content did.
  • Measured (dino PipelinedDualIssueCPU, sky130 liberty, 18 regions / 18.4k gates, -c opt): cold 1.90s (~5% over no-cache 1.72s), NoChange warm 0.57s — 15/18 regions hit; the floor is lg+liberty load plus the three uncacheable regions (DualIssueRegisterFile: anonymous register-file state; DualIssueHazardUnit: two genuinely symmetric outputs; the CPU top: a node-level comb cycle — the false-loop-through-forwarding shape the bit-blast fixpoint resolves per bit, which a node-granular digest cannot soundly key). Edit-one-module on hier_seq: children hit, only the edited def's regions re-map, all LEC-gated (lhd/tests/lhd_abc_incr_test.sh; digest contract pinned in pass/abc/abc_incr_test.cpp).
  • QoR on a hit replays the cached Region_qor row (gates/area/ delay/critical-source; the critical output rides its canonical rank), so qor.json totals are identical cold vs warm; the envelope gains "incremental":{"hits","misses","stored","uncacheable"} — the NoChange answer both commercial flows miss is hits==regions in O(#regions) lookups.
  • B's parallel half deferred deliberately: ABC's frame is a process-global singleton (one Abc_Start, one Liberty load, one current-network slot), so parallel mapping means the lec fork pattern — but a mapper child's result is a GRAPH (read-back into rb.body), not a verdict blob: it needs per-child GraphLibrary saves + parent load_merge + srcid/QoR/refusal merging, and arm_child_share divides the memory budget by N against an ABC peak factor measured at 2.5–29.6x — parallelism could refuse regions that pass sequentially. The cache already removes the repeated work; fork-parallel is a follow-up with this note as its map.
  • Caveats: the cache dir is single-writer (no cross-process lock, the formal_cache assumption); it grows without GC (content-addressed, so stale bodies are only disk); the salt constant (abc-incr-v1) must be bumped when the mapper read-back or digest changes shape — a source-hash genrule like //lhd:formal_salt is the eventual fix.

Subtasks — 0/A/C landed, B half, D/E/F pending

  • 0. ABC memory admission (prerequisite). Prevent a single large region — especially an intentional flat region — from exhausting physical memory and taking down the machine. Before translation, cheaply scan operation kinds and widths to estimate the region's bit-level ABC work (bitwise, mux and register work scale with width; add/compare with width plus an operation factor; multiply with the input-width product). LGraph node count alone is not a memory estimate: ABC bit-blasts wide operations and temporarily holds several network forms plus LiveHD's per-bit maps.

    During the real translation, pause after approximately 5% of the estimated work (not 5% of LGraph nodes), measure actual RSS growth and ABC-object production, and project both completed-translation memory and the later mapping peak. The projection is conceptually rss_before + sampled_growth * total_work / sampled_work, followed by a conservatively calibrated mapping-peak factor. Continue checking RSS while translating. The bytes-per-object and mapping factor must be calibrated from measured peak RSS; 32 bytes may seed the low-level object estimate but is not a safe per-LGraph-node contract.

    Admission uses physical memory only — never swap — subtracts current RSS (the loaded LGraph is already substantial), and reserves OS/process headroom (initial policy: the greater of 2 GiB or 20% of physical RAM). Permit an explicit pass.abc.memory_budget_mb=N for reproducible hosts and CI. If the conservative peak exceeds the effective budget, delete the partial ABC network, emit no partial successful result, and exit nonzero before running the synthesis commands. The diagnostic reports region/color, current RSS, physical budget, sampled fraction, projected translation and peak, then recommends recoloring with smaller regions. pass.abc.allow_oversize=true acknowledges and bypasses both admission checks; record that override prominently in the log and result. This guard applies to every coloring, not only flat.
  • A. Partition identity + signature cache — LANDED 2026-07-16 (see the landed section). Resolved content-addressed: the digest IS the identity, so the planned name-set tracking, re-assignment rule and re-partition trigger were never needed — a region "survives an RTL edit" exactly when its content did, and color renumbering/nid shifts/port renames are invisible by construction.
  • B. Parallel per-partition synthesis — aggregation half landed. Per-region QoR rows + incremental counters ride qor.json/the envelope. The PARALLEL scheduler is deferred (fork-cost note in the landed section): ABC's process-global frame forces the lec fork pattern, but a mapper child's result is a graph, not a verdict blob — per-child output libraries + GraphLibrary::load_merge + a memory budget split N ways against a 2.5–29.6x uncalibratable ABC peak factor.
  • C. Incremental resynthesis — LANDED 2026-07-16 (cache under --workdir, on by default; LEC-gated, byte-identical warm clones, lhd/tests/lhd_abc_incr_test.sh).
  • D. The FIB timing probe ("is X faster?"). Discover FIBs within a partition with the cone prover; on an edit, resynthesize only the touched cone with boundary delays pinned from the original run, and report the delay delta. Ephemeral: no stitch, no area accounting, no P&R.
  • E. Partition refinement policy. Grow a partition when its critical path spans input→output; split when it exceeds the 1k–5k gate band. NOTE (2026-07-16): that band is PHYSICAL gates and is not the unit 2c-color-size's min=/max= window uses — its GE metric measured 421 GE/node on XSCore (7.43e9 GE total), so pass.color's "1k-5k GE" histogram bucket is a far smaller region than this band means. Reaching a real 1k–5k gate grain needs either a calibrated GE→gate factor or post-ABC area feedback (which this task has and 2c does not). Cache the resulting partition decisions so the iteration is paid once.
  • F. Validation — and the area gap. Anubis-style change insertion (ch.3: 144 changes over dlx/alpha/fpu/mor1kx/or1200), LEC-gated. Measure area, which this lineage never did. Note ch.3's standing result: both commercial flows fail to detect a no-op edit (24 of the 144 changes are NoChange by construction), burning ~106s re-synthesizing a design that did not change — a digest-keyed cache answers those in O(#partitions) hash lookups, against a published baseline.

Open questions

  • Partition stability under edits that add/remove logic — how much drift before a re-partition is forced, and does a re-partition invalidate all signatures?
  • Does the probe need a sound bound ("never claims faster when it is not") or only a good estimate? The pruning role suggests a one-sided guarantee is what matters.
  • Cumulative degradation: every published change was applied independently against the original. A real session applies edits in sequence — the re-baseline policy is unknown and unmeasured.
  • Does partitioned synthesis cost measurable area/Fmax vs flat on today's flow? Never measured in 2017; now cheap to answer, and it decides the coloring policy.

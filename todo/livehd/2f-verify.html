P1 — assume-form discipline on the verify path (the "working with assert/assume" half)

Current reality (code survey 2026-07-12; the contract below is settled, the implementation is NOT there yet): the verify engine treats ANY kind=assume as a free environment constraint asserted at EVERY cycle including the reset prologue, never a proof obligation (pass/lec/query.cpp:4762-4777; inou/prp/formal_blocks.hpp:25-26 documents the V2-subset deferral). A formal-block assume over an INTERNAL register is silently accepted "in force" and can power a fake inductive PROVEN of its companion assert (reproduced 2026-07-12) — the only breadcrumb is the generic per-assume "verdicts are conditional on it" line. assume_nocheck_formal / assume_nocheck_synth are REJECTED in verify blocks (the extractor is called with allow_nocheck=false, lhd/lhd_kernel_formal.cpp:2816; only LEC opts in at :1618), and the "under N assume(s)" disclosures are LEC-only (disclose_lec_helpers, lhd_kernel_formal.cpp:271-284). The end-of-run vacuity checkSat (contradictory set ⇒ all Proven demoted to Unknown, query.cpp:5156-5183) is the sole backstop and stays.

  • Source 1: learned-literal recurrence (cheap — the solver already computed it). Harvest getLearnedLiterals(INPUT); strip cycle prefixes; a literal recurring at many cycles ("r5_st ≤ 3 at cycles 2..9") is an invariant candidate "st ≤ 3". Keep only literals over named user signals (internal temps can't become source code); word-level facts map back through encoder naming → LGraph signals → Pyrope names.
  • Source 2: register/state templates targeted at the pain. Where difficulty mass / timeout cores point, instantiate hardware-invariant templates over the registers in the hard asserts' cones: range bounds (seeded from BMC model values), one-hot state encodings, mutual exclusion of valid bits, register-pair equality, count == popcount(valids). Soundness rules (adversarially reviewed; the non-negotiables)

  • A. Assume-at-proven-horizon. A fact proven for cycles ≤ k may be assumed at cycles ≤ k while checking anything at bound ≤ k — but "proven" means per-cycle UNSAT verdicts (a tlimit'd sweep can be "proven at 2..5, Unknown at 6"; assuming the Unknown cycle is a real masking channel), and the assumption must be the exact term proven (X-mask and width-fit included).
  • B. Raising the bound k→k′ > k: clip assumptions to cycles ≤ k (free, always sound) or re-prove at k′ first. Assuming beyond the proven horizon can retroactively prune traces whose violation is at cycle 2 — strictly worse than not assuming at all. A base-SAT vacuity check does not detect partial pruning.
  • C. Circularity: same-cycle mutual assumption is unsound. Sound discipline = assume siblings only at cycles strictly earlier than the cycle being checked (McMillan's circular rule; Goldberg et al.'s JA-verification). With unproven mutual assumptions the guarantee is for the conjunction/first-failure only.
  • D. Learned-literal provenance: cvc5 does not tag scope provenance, so literals learned while an obligation was pushed may depend on it. Mining harvests only literals collected with no obligation pushed (level-0 provenance) over named symbols.
  • E. k-induction strengthening: the step case starts in arbitrary unreachable states — BMC-to-k facts may never be step-frame assumptions. Sound forms only: BMC discharges base cases; helpers assumed in the step must be true invariants (sidecar) or folded into the conjunctive obligation. Base depth ≥ induction depth used.

cvc5 1.3.4 API inventory (verified against the vendored headers)

  • checkSatAssuming (cvc5.h:5694) + push/pop — the engine substrate; incremental is default-on in 1.3.x.
  • getLearnedLiterals(modes::LearnedLitType) (cvc5.h:6156; gate produce-learned-literals) — unit facts entailed by the current assertions; INPUT mode = input-vocabulary, the minable class. This is why CNF-level solvers can't do the mining job — clauses over integer variables cannot be lifted back to word-level assumes.
  • getDifficulty() (cvc5.h:6029; gate produce-difficulty) — per-input-assertion lemma/conflict participation; callable right after any checkSat, including timeout. The scheduler's ranking signal (currently unused repo-wide).
  • getTimeoutCore() / getTimeoutCoreAssuming() (cvc5.h:6063/6101; knob timeout-core-timeout) — a subset of assertions that alone reproduces the timeout; the landed minetimeout diagnosis uses this.
  • getUnsatCore() (gate produce-unsat-cores) — which assumed facts a proof actually used ⇒ prune useless assumes, rank mined candidates.
  • getInterpolant/getAbduct — SyGuS-enumerative underneath; not credible on BMC-sized bit-vector formulas; do not plan invariant mining on them.
  • No solver-state serialization exists (Solver non-copyable). Cross-process = SMT-LIB text round-trip.
  • All of the above are experimental — probe options at startup (getOptionNames), degrade gracefully (mining off) rather than crash; a cvc5 upgrade may change them (solver version is in the cache key).

Traps

  • tlimit-per is per-checkSat — only the global TOTAL budget prevents (N+1)×timeout runs.
  • Deepening after assuming — on --deepen, frontier facts are already horizon-clipped by construction; sidecar facts are inductive so exempt; anything bounded from the cache must be re-proven at the new bound before reuse (rule B).
  • Symbol-name determinism — encode-order-stable naming or the cache keys churn (completeness, not soundness: a rename is a miss, never a wrong bind).
  • New Prop_result fields need codec extension — serialize_verify/deserialize_verify (pass/lec/query.cpp:434-536); new Witness_trace fields need put_trace/get_trace (query.cpp:230/:308). Either way the fork path silently drops unserialized fields.
